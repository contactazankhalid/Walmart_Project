## Walmart Sales Data Analysis (SQL)
# Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing historical sales data from Walmart to identify high-performing branches, product trends, and customer behavior. By answering critical business questions, the project demonstrates the use of SQL for Retail Analytics, helping stakeholders understand operational efficiency and revenue shifts.

# Technical Skills Demonstrated
Aggregations & Grouping: Using SUM(), AVG(), MIN(), and MAX() to summarize large datasets.

Complex Case Logic: Categorizing time-based data into shifts (Morning, Afternoon, Evening).

Advanced Ranking: Using Window Functions (e.g., RANK(), ROW_NUMBER()) to identify top performers per branch/category.

Financial Modeling: Calculating profit margins and Year-over-Year (YoY) revenue growth ratios.

Data Cleaning: Handling date/time formats for time-series analysis.

# Key Business Insights
1. Payment & Transaction Analysis
Payment Methods: Analyzed the distribution of transactions and quantities sold across different payment modes to identify customer preferences.

Branch-Level Trends: Identified the most common payment method used within each specific branch.

# 2. Performance Metrics (Rating & Profit)
Quality Control: Found the highest-rated product categories per branch and calculated the average, minimum, and maximum ratings per city.

Financial Health: Calculated the total profit generated by each product category to determine the most lucrative segments.

# 3. Operational Efficiency
Time-Series Analysis: Determined the "Busiest Day" for each branch based on transaction volume to optimize staffing.

Shift Analysis: Categorized sales into Morning, Afternoon, and Evening shifts to track peak sales periods throughout the day.

4. Strategic Growth (Advanced)
Revenue Growth/Decline: Identified the top 5 branches experiencing the highest revenue decrease ratio compared to the previous year. This analysis is crucial for identifying underperforming locations requiring strategic intervention.
